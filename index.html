As a first-generation Latino college student, I wish to create a professional career and work hard to achieve all my goals. I believe in one main value that if followed will help me overcome obstacles and be the best version of muyself in whatever work setting I am in.

Throughout history, and in all aspects of society, it has been apparent that teamwork and team spirit are of utmost importance when success is being sought after. It is key to have good team spirit, and for a team to move like a well-oiled machine. Teamwork can manifest itself in our most routine exercises of daily life. In school, teamwork makes projects run smoothly and easily. Diverse viewpoints and opinions are fundamental to success as a team. This has been a cardinal principle throughout my life, especially in my education and young college career. Now as a rising sophomore at the University of California at Riverside, I reflect on the value of teamwork and appreciate its significance by way of two prime examples. First, when I was struggling with Calculus in the winter, I sought help from my classmates. We formed a study group and worked as a team through the complex mathematical formulas and principles, to gain a better understanding and mastery of the material. Lastly, school-related team spirit does not always have to take the very specific form of a solely academic setting. Living away from home at school, I found a support system in friendships. A reliable group of friends is the ultimate form of team spirit to drive an individual to success. I was lucky enough to find an encouraging and bolstering friend group that backed me up in everyday things like getting homework done and sleeping enough, to things such as working out for my well being and talking about my problems. The reinforcement and teamwork my friends provided me with in my first year of college will allow me to achieve my goals both in the short and long term, leading to my definition of success in life. After college, I envision using my education and degree to work on the business, administrative, and/or managerial side of collegiate or professional sports. The teamwork principles that are important to me now, will be important to me when I embark upon my career. I believe that by trusting and participating in teamwork intelligently, that will help me get closer to my goals and be a part of something bigger than myself. In conclusion, I wish to help others in my own way, and a solid team spirit will allow me to do that through my objectives and goals.
